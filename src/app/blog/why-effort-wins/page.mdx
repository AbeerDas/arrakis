# Why small, visible effort beats a polished generic email

_June 18, 2026_

> **Editor's note (draft):** This is launch placeholder content. Per our policy,

> their permission**, before publishing. The `[bracketed]` names below are

There's an irony at the center of this product: we generate a strong baseline
cold email for you, and yet the thing we most believe about outreach is that the
emails which actually work are the ones that show a little real, specific effort.

A great baseline email gets you in the door. What gets a reply is evidence that
you looked closely and did something only a real person would bother to do.

## The handmade object

[Name] wanted to work at a small hardware startup. Instead of a cover letter,
they mailed a physical object — a rough prototype of an accessory for the
company's product, made over a weekend. It was not polished. It was unmistakably
made _for them_. They got a call within two days.

## The 90-second product teardown

[Name] recorded a short screen-share walking through one concrete improvement to
the company's onboarding flow — not a critique, a fix, with the before and after.
No "I'm passionate about your mission." Just: here is a thing I noticed, and here
is how I'd change it.

## Explaining fit out loud

[Name] sent a 60-second video answering one question: why this specific role, at
this specific company, for them. Founders skim text. A short, sincere video that
proves you understand the job is hard to ignore.

## Just suggesting an improvement

The lowest-effort version still works: [Name] emailed a two-line note pointing
out a broken link and a confusing label on the company's signup page, with the
fix. It read as "I already act like I work here."

## The throughline

None of these were long. None were polished in the cover-letter sense. Every one
of them was **specific** and showed **visible effort** — something the recipient
could tell took real time and attention.

Use the generated email as your floor, not your ceiling. Start from a sharp,
specific baseline, then add the one human touch that only you would think to add.
